Little surprise! I bought this chocolate in Quito... yet it is Argentinian, from the Misiones province bordering Paraguay.
It appears to be a limited edition, as the Arapegua cooperative—better known for mate production—no longer offers it in their shop.

It's a pleasant chocolate, fairly sweet at 60%. At first it seems ordinary, but with attention one finds touches of dried fruits—raisins, apricots—and hints of Alicante nougat.


### Evaluation

_Originality_: 3.5  
_Fineness_: 3.5  
_Comforting_: 4.5  
_Intensity_: 4  
_Overall impression_: 3.5

**Final evaluation**: 19/25
